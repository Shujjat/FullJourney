**Introduction**
Coulomb's Law is a fundamental concept in physics that explains the relationship between two charged particles. It is a crucial tool for understanding electric forces and charges.

**References**

* Feynman, R. P. (1963). The Feynman Lectures on Physics.
* Halliday, D., Resnick, R., & Walker, J. (2013). Fundamentals of Physics.
* Griffiths, D. J. (2008). Introduction to Electrodynamics.

**Detailed Response**
Coulomb's Law states that the force between two charged particles is proportional to the product of their charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them. The law can be mathematically represented as:

F = k \* (q1 \* q2) / r^2

where F is the force, k is Coulomb's constant, q1 and q2 are the charges of the two particles, and r is the distance between them.

**Example 1: Two identical charged spheres**
Suppose we have two identical charged spheres with a charge of +3 μC each. If they are placed 5 cm apart, what is the force of attraction between them?

Using Coulomb's Law, we can calculate the force:

F = k \* (3 μC) ^2 / (0.05 m) ^2

F ≈ 9.8 × 10^-7 N

**Example 2: A charged particle and a neutral sphere**
A charged particle with a charge of -5 μC is placed 1 cm away from a neutral metal sphere. What is the force of attraction between them?

Using Coulomb's Law, we can calculate the force:

F = k \* (-5 μC) \* (0) / (0.01 m) ^2

F ≈ 0 N

**Related Questions with Answers**

Q: How does Coulomb's Law relate to other fundamental forces in physics?
A: Coulomb's Law is one of the four fundamental forces in physics, along with gravity, electromagnetism, and the strong nuclear force.

Q: What are some applications of Coulomb's Law in everyday life?
A: Coulomb's Law has many practical applications, such as electric motors, generators, and transformers.
